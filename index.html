Areas of Research

What we study

Two intertwined programs: the foundations of an alternative model architecture, and its first application to structured 3D generation.

Foundational model research

Semantic Gaussian Splatting (SGS) — an alternative architecture to the transformer, where one Gaussian primitive carries meaning, appearance, and behaviour.

Alpha-compositing expressiveness
Studying volume-rendering's weighting equation as the mechanism inside LLMs — proven a strict superset of softmax attention.
Gaussian-splat blobs
Splats as units of knowledge, encoding semantic, visual, and physical properties in one primitive.
Transmittance-based rendering
One substrate for both semantic and physical computation, governed by accumulated transmittance.
Physical-property encoding
Extending the primitive with material semantics so the scene that renders also simulates.

Prompt-to-3D scene generation

Turning natural language into structured, editable 3D — named parts instead of a single opaque blob.

Semantic extraction
A language model reads the meaning of a prompt and the entities it implies.
Recursive decomposition
The prompt becomes a composition tree: each named concept expands into sub-parts down to individual primitives.
Gaussian-splat rendering
Each leaf renders as positioned splats; the tree itself is the editable, queryable representation.
Export & reuse
Scenes export to standard 3D formats, so parts can be edited and reused rather than regenerated.
Products & Models

What we build

Models and applications that put the research to work, from a browser-sized base model to a full text-to-3D pipeline.

Models

Planck Model

A nano-scale base model — ~100M parameters, trained on Wikipedia. The reference SGS language model and the testbed for blob-based retrieval.

Hertz Model · In Progress

The next-scale SGS language model — targeting ~1 billion parameters. The base that Raum will run on.

Raum Model

The text-to-3D model: prompt-conditioned generation of Gaussian-splat scenes via recursive semantic-to-geometric decomposition.

Klang Model

SGS multimodality: encoding audio as Gaussian splats. Probing whether the same primitive that renders meaning and geometry can also synthesize sound.

Applications

Raum Application

The interactive pipeline — grammatic decomposition, semantic encoder, and real-time rendering of named, editable parts, with .ply export to game engines and 3D tools.

Satz Application

Browser-native chat and generation on the SGS base model — Planck running locally, Hertz served via API.

Klang Application

The audio-synthesis surface of SGS: turning Gaussian-splat representations into sound — studio, voice, and field.

What we've written

Formal and applied work from the lab. Where claims are mathematical, they are machine-verified in Lean 4.

Submitted to JMLR · 2026
On the Expressiveness of Alpha-Compositing: A Strict Superset of Softmax Attention

Proves that every softmax-attention weighting can be reproduced exactly by alpha-compositing, but not the reverse — alpha-compositing also yields hard sparsity and sub-unity weight sums. The full result is machine-verified in Lean 4 with Mathlib, zero unproven assertions.

Recursive Semantic-to-Geometric Decomposition via Gaussian Splatting

Introduces RSGD: pairing a small language model (the Decomposer) with a Gaussian-splat renderer to turn prompts into hierarchical composition trees that bottom out at individual 3D Gaussian primitives — structured, editable scenes instead of monolithic geometry.

Physical Gaussians: Extending the SGS Primitive with Material Semantics

Extends the Gaussian primitive with a physical-property embedding so the same scene that drives rendering also drives physics — correlating what something means, what it looks like, and how it behaves. Six of its formal claims are proven in Lean 4.
